Graphic Design Projects

This page consists of the previous graphic design projects I've completed while getting my Visual Communications Diploma. I feel these projects show my skills as a graphic designer as well as projects I have done as a freelancer.





Sweden T-Shirt

View Project

Class: Communication Design 2, Summer 2018

Project: For this project, my class was tasked with making a t-shirt design for DMACC's International Year. This year the country was Sweden. So we needed to do some research on Sweden, we needed to make 20 thumbnails for this project, then a rough sketch, we also needed to choose what fonts and colors we were going to use and explain why

What I Did: I decided to focus on the architecture of Sweden for 2 reasons, 1)I love architecture (I actually wanted to be an architect when I was a sophomore in high school) and 2)most of my classmates were doing nature themed designs so I wanted to do something different. There were a many buildings that were special to Sweden but the one that stuck out the most to me was the Twisted Skyscraper. So that's what I used as my inspiration for this design. I traced the structure and used Sweden's colors to fill and shade it

What I learned: During this project I learned that if I make thumbnails, the font and the colors I want to use, then designing it on the computer is pretty easy because I already hav everything planned out

Tools I Used: I used InDesign for this project


Sweden Poster

View Project

Class: Communication Design 2, Summer 2018

Project: This project is a continuation of DMACC's International Year. For this project we needed to make a poster for Sweden. The poster had to be a different concept than the shirt, it had to have 10 thumbnails, a rough sketch and the fonts and colors we chose and why

What I Did: Since we had to use a different concept, I decided to use a deer and mountain ranges for my poster. I wanted this poster to be elegant and minimalist. I used a darker blue and a golden yellow for my colors, which I felt were elegant. I did a simple outline of a deer and I used different shaded of the golden yellow to show depth and dimension. I decides to add in a pyramid of squares in the top corners because I feel like they guide your eyes down to the rest of the poster

What I learned: The main thing I learned for this project is how to get a few good ideas for the same topic and make those ideas work. When we first got this project, I had no clue what I was going to do, but after I did a little bit more research I quickly came up with 2 ideas that would both work well for this project

Tools I used: I used Illustrator for tracing the deer and I used InDesign for the rest of the poster

Culinary Magazine Cover

View Project

Class: Communication Design 2, Summer 2018

Project: My class was given the task of making a new Culinary Magazine (we had 4 pages we needed to redo). The first page we were redoing is the front cover page. We needed to have "Iowa Culinary Institute" on the front, we also needed to have "From DMACC Classrooms to Iowa Restaurants" on the front as well and we needed to chose an image on for the front (we could either make our own or use one of the many images that were provided for us to use)

What I did: I decided to take a minimalist approach to the cover. I wanted it to have a nice elegant look to it as well. I chose a teal and a darker teal for the colors because they went well together and I would be able to make something stand out if need be. I chose the image on the front because it went very well with the colors and it was simple as well

What I Learned: I learned how to tie everything together on a front cover page. Make sure the colors, the image, and even the font and the font colors all go together and don't take anything away from each other

Tools I Used: I used InDesign for this page


Culinary Magazine Dinner List

View Project

Class: Communication Design 2, Summer 2018

Project: This is the second page we needed to redo for the Culinary Magazine. For this page we needed to format the dinner list. Make sure that the dates are readable but the page shouldn't be crammed together, images are to be used, and make sure the page relates to the front cover

What I did: I decided to make the heading and the footer in the same dark teal that's on the front cover. Then I decided to make horizontal lines on the sides of each dinner in the lighter teal color because that acts like bullet points for each event date and I made the Special Events header stick out more because people love looking at special events (it also relates back to the header on the front cover as well). I added in the gallery of pictures at the bottom because I liked all those pictures and I felt they worked well with the page and the colors go well together

What I Learned: I learned how to make a page relate to the front cover and I learned how to format dates so it will all fit on the same page without looking too crowded, I also learned how to use font size as hierarchy

Tools I Used: I used InDesign for this page

Culinary Magazine Toque Society

View Project

Class: Communication Design 2, Summer 2018

Project: This page is for the Toque Society. This outlines what each level requires (contribution amount) and what you get with each level. This page has, by far, the most text so no pictures were required for this page. Although we needed to make sure that all the text fit on the page without it being unreadable

What I did: The first thing I did for this page was make sure I could get all the text on but still be at a size that people could read. After I got that done then I styled it to fit the rest of the magazine. I figured that if I styled it first, then I wouldn't be able to fit all the text on or I'd need to change the style a lot to accommodate all the text

What I Learned: The biggest struggle for me on this project was getting all the text to fit, so I learned how to make the text fit while still being readable

Tools I Used: I used InDesign for this page

Acne

View Project: Girl View Original Girl Picture View Project: Guy View Original Guy Picture

Class: Photoshop, Spring 2018

Project: For this project, my class was tasked with covering up blemishes on pictures of people. My teacher decided to give us pictures of people with acne on them because removing acne is the most common blemish cover up that people do. MY teacher also wanted us to chose 2 pictures to edit from the ones she provided for us

What I Did: I decided to edit a picture of a girl and of a guy because I wanted to know how to edit both. For both pictures it was a little difficult to make sure their skin was even through out the picture and with the guy it was a little bit more tricky to clear the blemishes that were under his mustache. During the editing process, I flipped back and forth between the original picture and the one I edited so I could see how the blemishes looked and if they were covered up enough. It was also nice to see the final picture compared to the original picture when I was done editing

What I Learned: I learned how to cover and clean the blemishes on pictures, which has come in handy down the road when I've needed to edit my own pictures

Tools I Used: I used Photoshop for this project

Coffee Designs Card

View Project

Project: This was one of the first projects I did on my own. I wanted some experience making a business card for a company instead of just for me (so far any business card I made had been for me). First off I needed a company to make a card for, have their logo on the card, have a color scheme and have a slogan or info on the card too.

What I Did: I decided to make a card for a fictitious company. I made up the Coffee Designs name when I was younger and I added the name to any gift I made (it had become a trademark of my gifts). Coffee Designs is such a versatile name it could be a company for essentially anything. Which is why I keep using it. In this case Coffee Designs is a handmade jewelry making company. I chose the color scheme because these colors aren't unusual for handmade jewelry making companies. I designed the logo so that it would be simple enough to be put on a metal stamp and stamped into metal (if a logo is too complex not all the details will show up). I also came up with the slogan on the back. I wanted this card to be modern and simple.

What I Learned: I learned how to make a business card that reflects the company it's for

Tools I Used: I used Illustrator and InDesign for this project
