Graphic Design Projects

This page consists of the previous graphic design projects I've completed while getting my Visual Communications Diploma. I feel these projects show my skills as a graphic designer





Treasure Island Music Festival

View Project

Class: InDesign 1, Fall 2017

Project: I was tasked with making a poster for the Treasure Island Music Festival. The poster needs to say when and where it is, as well as the bands that are taking part in the festival. The Treasure Island logo also needed to be included on the poster

What I Did: I chose the image inside the logo because it makes me think of a sunset on the island (which also allows the logo to stand out). I decided to use multiple shades of blue because it reminds me of the ocean, since islands are surrounded by water

What I Learned: I learned how to put an image inside a logo, how to use various fonts (and make sure those fonts look good together), and how to separate text with symbols

Tools I Used: For this project I used InDesign

Sweden T-Shirt

View Project

Class: Communication Design 2, Summer 2018

Project: For this project, my class was tasked with making a t-shirt design for DMACC's International Year. This year the country was Sweden. So we needed to do some research on Sweden, we needed to make 20 thumbnails for this project, then a rough sketch, we also needed to choose what fonts and colors we were going to use and explain why

What I Did: I decided to focus on the architecture of Sweden for 2 reasons, 1)I love architecture (I actually wanted to be an architect when I was a sophomore in high school) and 2)most of my classmates were doing nature themed designs so I wanted to do something different. There were a many buildings that were special to Sweden but the one that stuck out the most to me was the Twisted Skyscraper. So that's what I used as my inspiration for this design. I traced the structure and used Sweden's colors to fill and shade it

What I learned: During this project I learned that if I make thumbnails, the font and the colors I want to use, then designing it on the computer is pretty easy because I already hav everything planned out

Tools I Used: I used InDesign for this project

Sweden Poster

View Project

Class: Communication Design 2, Summer 2018

Project: This project is a continuation of DMACC's International Year. For this project we needed to make a poster for Sweden. The poster had to be a different concept than the shirt, it had to have 10 thumbnails, a rough sketch and the fonts and colors we chose and why

What I Did: Since we had to use a different concept, I decided to use a deer and mountain ranges for my poster. I wanted this poster to be elegant and minimalist. I used a darker blue and a golden yellow for my colors, which I felt were elegant. I did a simple outline of a deer and I used different shaded of the golden yellow to show depth and dimension. I decides to add in a pyramid of squares in the top corners because I feel like they guide your eyes down to the rest of the poster

What I learned: The main thing I learned for this project is how to get a few good ideas for the same topic and make those ideas work. When we first got this project, I had no clue what I was going to do, but after I did a little bit more research I quickly came up with 2 ideas that would both work well for this project

Tools I used: I used Illustrator for tracing the deer and I used InDesign for the rest of the poster

Ninja Sushi Brochure

View Project

Class: InDesign 1, Fall 2017

Project: For this project, I needed to make a menu brochure for Ninja Sushi. The brochure needed to have all the menu items and it's prices, the Ninja Sushi logo needed to be included, as well as an indicator of the menu items that were vegetarian, spicy, and a Ninja Sushi special

What I Did: Spacing was very important for this project, there was quite a bit of information we needed to add but the brochure couldn't be too crammed or else it would be hard for people to read and it wouldn't look nice either. So, planning was a very big help in completing this project. Once I knew where I wanted everything to go, making the brochure was easy. I used the colors blue and grey because they are simple colors that go well together and can stand out when need be

What I Learned: During this project I learned how important planning your layout is, especially when space can be an issue. I learned how to use and implement tabs in InDesign, I also learned how to put an image in front of text (while not messing up the spacing of the menu)

Tools I Used: For this project I used Illustrator for the logo (changing the color) and I used InDesign to make the menu

Eat Raw Newsletter

View Project

Class: InDesign 2, Spring 2018

Project: For this project, I was tasked with making the Eat Raw! newsletter. There is specific text that needs to be used, header and footers needed to be used as well as consistent, pictures needed to be added in(lots of pictures were provided), some text needed to continue onto another page, some text needed to be set around pictures and there needed to be a mailer on the back page

What I did: I wanted this project to have an eat raw feel to it so I mainly used various shades of green and brown for the newsletter. Recipe Book Front & Back Cover

View Project

Class: InDesign 1, Fall 2017

Project: For this project, we needed to make a recipe book with 8 recipes in it. The only things that were provided for us are the tables in the front of the recipe book and the outline of the project. We needed to find recipes, pictures, make the front and back covers and style everything so it all fits and looks good
What I did: I decided to choose recipes from my childhood. These are ones I grew up using and each one has a story behind it. I decided to use light green, light blue, brown and white for my colors because they go well together and they provide a nice, calming tone to the recipe book. I wanted to color coordinate each section because that allows the pages to relate to each other and that's also why I used the same format for each page. This project was one of my favorites to make because I got to relive some of my most fond memories while putting this project together

What I Learned: I learned how to style a recipe book and format each page so that it looks like the rest of the pages but it also is unique in its own way. Spacing did become an issue for some of the pages but it helped me figure out how to make everything fit while still keeping the same format and having the font be big enough for people to easily read

Tools I Used: I used InDesign for this project


Acne

View Project: Girl View Original Girl Picture View Project: Guy View Original Guy Picture

Class: Photoshop, Spring 2018

Project: For this project, my class was tasked with covering up blemishes on pictures of people. My teacher decided to give us pictures of people with acne on them because removing acne is the most common blemish cover up that people do. MY teacher also wanted us to chose 2 pictures to edit from the ones she provided for us

What I Did: I decided to edit a picture of a girl and of a guy because I wanted to know how to edit both. For both pictures it was a little difficult to make sure their skin was even through out the picture and with the guy it was a little bit more tricky to clear the blemishes that were under his mustache. During the editing process, I flipped back and forth between the original picture and the one I edited so I could see how the blemishes looked and if they were covered up enough. It was also nice to see the final picture compared to the original picture when I was done editing

What I Learned: I learned how to cover and clean the blemishes on pictures, which has come in handy down the road when I've needed to edit my own pictures

Tools I Used: I used Photoshop for this project
