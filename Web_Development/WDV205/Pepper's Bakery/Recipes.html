On this page we will provide the recipes to some of our most requested/bought items. There is one recipe for cookies, pies, cakes, cupcakes and pastries. You can half the servings, keep it the same, or you can double the servings.



Ice Cream Cupcakes



Makes 6 Cupcakes

Prep Time: 5 minutes
Total Time: 2 hours 30 minutes
Difficulty: Easy
Servings: 6 cupcakes

  • 1 box chocolate cake mix (plus ingredients to make it)
  • 3 cups (or 1 1/2 pints) ice cream (choose your favorite kind!)
  • 1 cup whipped cream
  • 1/2 cups melted semisweet chocolate chips
  • 1/4 cups of any topping you would like


  1. Preheat the oven to 350 degrees F and line a muffin tin with 6 cupcake liners. Prepare the cupcake batter according to the directions on the box and put a quarter of the batter in the liners (You can freeze the leftover batter for up to a month)
  2. Bake about 15 minutes or until a toothpick comes out clean, allow the cupcakes to cool before putting in ice cream
  3. Use a melon baller (or a spoon) and scoop out the middles of each cupcake (you are going to create a well in the cupcake). Trim the bottoms of the tops so they are flat, then set them aside
  4. Fill the cupcakes with ice cream and cover with the tops, place in the freezer for about 1 1/2 - 2 hours
  5. When the cupcakes are firm, add the whipped cream, drizzle on the melted chocolate, and add any other toppings

Giant Molten Chocolate Cake



Makes 4 Servings

Prep Time: 5 minutes
Total Time: 1 hour
Difficulty: Easy
Servings: 4

  • 1 box chocolate cake mix (plus ingredients to make it)
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 8 oz dark chocolate
  • Cocoa powder (for dusting)


  1. Preheat oven to 350 degrees F
  2. In a large bowl, prepare the cake mix according to the package instructions
  3. Pour the batter into a well-greased small (7 1/2 by 3 inches) bundt pan (You can freeze the leftover batter for up to a month)
  4. Bake for 18-20 minutes or until a toothpick comes out clean
  5. Heat the heavy cream in a small saucepan until it is just boiling
  6. Put the chocolate in a Advanced bowl and carefully pour the hot cream over the chocolate. Mix until the chocolate is completely melted and smooth
  7. Flip the bundt cake over onto a serving plate
  8. Pour all the chocolate ganache into the hole in the middle, filling it up and spilling over the sides (ganache should cover the cake)
  9. Dust the cocoa powder over the cake and allow it to sit about 30 minutes before you serve it

Mixed Berry Pie



Makes 4 Servings

Prep Time: 25 minutes
Total Time: 1 hours 50 minutes
Difficulty: Advanced
Servings: 4

  • 1 1/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/8 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup cold, cubed butter
  • 3 to 4 tablespoons ice water

    • Filling:

  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1/8 cup cornstarch
  • Dash of salt
  • 2 tablespoons and 2 teaspoons water
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cinnamon (optional)
  • 1/2 cup fresh blueberries
  • 1/2 cup fresh raspberries
  • 1/2 cup halved fresh strawberries
  • 3 tablespoons fresh blackberries
  • 1/2 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 tablespoon butter


  1. In a large bowl, mix flour and salt; cut in butter until crumbly. Gradually add in the ice water, tossing with a fork until the dough holds together when pressed. Divide in two (one should be slightly bigger than the other). Shape each section into a disk; wrap both in plastic. Refrigerate for 30 minutes or overnight
  2. For the filling, in a large saucepan, whisk sugar, cornstarch, salt, water and cinnamon (if you want cinnamon) until smooth; add in blueberries. Bring to a boil; cooke and stir for 2 minutes or until thickened. Cool slightly
  3. Preheat oven to 400 degrees F. gently fold raspberries, strawberries, blackberries and lemon juice into blueberry mixture. Lightly flour the counter, roll out the larger portion of dough to a 1/8-inch thick circle; transfer to a 5-inch pie plate. Trim pastry to 1/2-inch beyond rim of plate. Add the filling and dot with butter
  4. Roll the remaining dough to a 1/8-inch thick circle; cut into 1/2-inch wide strips. Arrange over filling in a lattice pattern. Trim and seal strips to edge of the bottom of the pastry; flute the edge. Bake for about 12 minutes
  5. Reduce oven setting to 350 degrees F, bake about 20-25 minutes or until the crust is golden brown and the filling is bubbly. Cool on a wire rack. Serve and enjoy

Soft Frosted Sugar Cookie



Makes 18 Cookies

Prep Time: 20 minutes
Total Time: 1 hour 20 minutes
Difficulty: Easy
Servings: 18 Cookies

  • 1/2 cup vegetable shortening
  • 1/2 cup white sugar
  • 1/8 cup milk
  • 1 egg
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda

    • Frosting:

  • 1/8 cup butter
  • 1 1/2 cups confectioners sugar
  • 3/4 teaspoons vanilla extract (or more to taste)
  • 1 tablespoon milk (or as needed)
  • Food coloring (optional)
  • Toppings (optional)


  1. Preheat oven to 350 degrees F
  2. Beat vegetable shortening, white sugar, 1/8 cup milk, eggs, and 1/2 teaspoon of vanilla extract together in a bowl. Whisk flour, salt and baking soda in a separate bowl. Slowly beat flour mixture into shortening mixture to make a smooth dough
  3. Roll dough out 1/4-inch thick on a floured work surface and cut into shapes. Arrange the cookies on baking sheets
  4. Bake in the preheated oven for about 20 minutes or until cookies are firm. Let cool on a wire rack
  5. Beat butter, confectioners sugar, 3/4 teaspoon vanilla extract and 1 tablespoon milk in a bowl with an electric mixer on high until frosting is spreadable and slightly fluffy. Add in food coloring and frost the cooled cookies. Add on toppings when you are done frosting

Cannolis



Makes 15 Cannolis

Prep Time: 45 minutes
Total Time: 3 hours 45 minutes
Difficulty: Advanced
Servings: 15 Cannolis

    Shells:

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/8 cup white sugar
  • 1/8 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1 1/2 tablespoons shortening
  • 1/2 egg
  • 1/2 egg yolk
  • 1/2 tablespoon distilled white vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon water
  • 1/2 egg white
  • 1 pint oil (for frying or as needed)

    • Filling:

  • 1/2 container ricotta cheese (16 ounces)
  • 1/4 cup confectioners sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon lemon zest (or to taste)
  • 2 ounces semisweet chocolate


  1. In a medium bowl, mix together the flour, sugar and cinnamon. Cut in the shortening until it is in pieces on larger than peas. Make a well in the center, and pour in the egg, egg yolk, vinegar and water. Mix with a fork until the dough becomes stiff, then finish by hand, kneading on a clean surface. Add more water if needed to incorporate all the dry ingredients. Knead for about 10 minutes, then cover and refrigerate for 1-2 hours
  2. Divide the dough into thirds, flatten each one just enough to get through a pasta machine. Roll the dough through successively thinner settings until you reach the thinnest setting. Dust lightly with flour if necessary. Place the sheet of dough on a lightly floured surface. Using a large glass or bowl, cut out 4 to 5 inch circles. Dust the circles with a light coating of flour. This will help removing the shells from the tubes later on. Roll dough around cannoli tubes, sealing the edge with a bit of egg white
  3. Heat the oil to 375 degrees F in a deep-fryer (or a deep heavy skillet). Fry shells on the tubes a few at a time for about 2 to 3 minutes, or until golden. Use tongs to turn as needed. Carefully remove using tongs and place on a cooling rack set over paper towels. Cool just long enough that you can handle the tubes, carefully twist the tube to remove the shells. Using a towel can help you get a better grip. Wash/wipe off the tubes and use them for more shells. Cooled shells can be placed in an airtight container and kept for up to 2 months. Fill the shells up to 1 hour before serving them
  4. To make the filling, stir together the ricotta cheese and confectioners sugar using a spoon. Fold in lemon zest and chocolate. Use a pastry bag to pipe into the shells, filling them from the center to one end, then doing the same from the other end. Dust with additional confectioners sugar and grated chocolate for garnish before serving

Oreo Balls

At the end of every year we make Oreo Balls for the holidays, they become one of our most anticipated and most sold items

Prep Time: 25 minutes
Total Time: 1 hour
Difficulty: Easy
Servings: 40 balls

Ingredients

  • 40 Oreos
  • 8 oz cream cheese (softened)
  • 16 oz semisweet chocolate
  • 16 oz white chocolate
  • Sprinkles (or any other topping you would like)

Directions

  1. Line a baking sheet with parchment paper
  2. Put cookies into a blender and crush the cookies. If you don't have a blender, put cookies in a plastic bag and crush with a rolling pin. Put 1/4 of the cookies aside (if you want to use as topping for later)
  3. In a Advanced bowl, combine the crushed oreos and softened cream cheese. Mix well (should be like a paste when you are done). Scoop out about 1 in balls, roll in your hand to smooth out, place on baking sheet. When done, place in freezer for 20 minutes
  4. While the oreo Balls are in the freezer, grab two bowls and place the semisweet chocolate in one bowl and the white chocolate in the other. Microwave each bowl for 30 seconds, stir, then repeat until chocolate is melted and smooth
  5. Dip each ball in the chocolate, then you can add whatever toppings you want!
