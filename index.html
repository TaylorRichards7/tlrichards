Front-End Developer

When I first went to DMACC I was going to get a Graphic Design Degree. I had taken some classes in high school and I really enjoyed the creative aspect of the field. My first semester I took an Intro HTML & CSS class (all graphic designers are required to take this class though most take it in the summer). Long story short going into the class I was not looking forward to it, but by the time I left I loved learning how to code - even if it was just the basics. The next semester I decide to take another web development class: Intro to JavaScript. This is the class that made me decide to transfer from graphic design to web development. At this point I only had 2 more classes I needed to take in order to get a Visual Communications diploma so I decided to take those classes. Once that fall semester began I had officially transferred to web development.

This summer I completed the Web Development degree. At first my plan was to finish the Web degree then go back and finish the Graphic Design degree - now my plan is to focus on Web Development and grow my skills and knowledge in the web field. I will keep this site up to date on what I have learned and the new projects I have completed.

I still do Graphic Design work from time to time, however nowadays the projects I do are freelance projects and not school related. There will be less school projects and more of my freelance projects on this site.
